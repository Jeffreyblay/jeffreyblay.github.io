### LiDAR Point Cloud Analysis with ArcGIS Pro

**Objective:** To estimate building heights using 3D LiDAR point cloud data to support spatial modeling and infrastructure assessment.

**Method:** Processed and analyzed LiDAR point cloud datasets in ArcGIS Pro to derive surface elevations and extract building height metrics.

**Outcome:** Produced accurate building height estimates for campus structures, enabling improved planning, visualization, and geospatial analysis.

### Urban Heat Vulnerability Analysis

**Objective:** To assess urban heat patterns and tree canopy coverage in New Haven, CT to support equitable urban planning and climate resilience efforts.

**Method:** Conducted block-level geospatial analysis by comparing street-tree data, land surface temperature, and NDVI with built-environment characteristics using ArcGIS tools.

**Outcome:** Generated actionable insights on urban heat exposure and tree canopy gaps, providing data-driven recommendations to inform tree-planting strategies and environmental justice initiatives.

### Integrated Field Mapping and Monitoring System

**Outcome:** Enhanced field efficiency, improved data accuracy and organization, and enabled real-time monitoring and preliminary spatial analysis for informed decision-making.

### Urban Building Density Mapping in Africa/Urban Infrastructure Data Engineering for AI

**Objective:** To analyze and model urban building infrastructure across African cities using AI-driven geospatial methods.

**Method:** Processed over 60,000 OSM building footprints into raster training masks using QGIS, ArcPy, and R to support deep learning model development.

**Outcome:** Generated high-quality building density datasets to enable accurate AI-based urban infrastructure mapping and analysis.

### Fire Emergency Response Optimization for Urban Areas

**Objective:** To analyze fire truck accessibility and optimize emergency response coverage in Accra-Ghana, with a focus on Accra Central Business District.

**Method:** Mapped optimal fire truck routes and analyzed station coverage against National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) standards (using ArcGIS Pro and google earth) to evaluate potential response time gaps.

**Outcome:** Identified areas where fire stations exceed recommended coverage distances, highlighting critical gaps that could delay emergency response and informing strategic placement improvements.
